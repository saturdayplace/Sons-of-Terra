--- layout: post title: The Beginning of the Xenos author: Xander ---

Most mercenary outfits will hire any pilot with a mech who wants in. It's hard to blame them; that kind of heavy equipment is hard to come by, and I don't know of a company that can't use the guns. But hiring pilots helter-skelter buys you the command of a helter-skelter unit. I'd rather my pilots have demonstrated a certain kind of character; the kind you don't find much in mercenaries. I don't have a name for it, and veterans tend to hide behind a ridiculous swaggering bravado so it's not easy to find.

Starting out, it was just me and Flint. Flint's the perfect example of what I'm talking about. Doesn't jump to conclusions, but makes quick decisions on the field. Drinks like a fish, but is the first one to the gym the next morning. To my knowledge, he's never lost even a single hand-to-hand bout. I'm almost certain his story about single-handedly taking out that platoon on New Kansas is either grossly overstated or entirely fabricated, because he only tells it when he's drunk. He cares about the men under his command in 'A' company. A lot, and they know it; they'd follow him down the gullet of Hades and swim the Styx to bring back Cerberus' heads. You don't find these guys just by hiring whomever shows up at your door.

And after Copper Flats, a mass of misguided or misbegotten miscreants streamed through the door. Most of the companies involved didn't have any real leadership remaining after the battle, and that has an effect on the younger ranks. Much as they all like to think they'd do better calling the shots, much as they brag to each other over drinks about what they'll do when their "turn comes," when given the chance most folk'll back away. It's intimidating to stare right in the face of real responsibility. The outfits involved in the Flats just lamely fell apart aftwards as the youth drifted to places they could count on. The middle ranks from those — guys who'd be non-coms in a conventional army — were left stranded without a place to work.

Flint and I spent the days after the flats doing maintenance on our rides in the Titans'1 garage. Two days after the battle, a pair of these vets showed up and asked for a job. Neither of us was in a position to speak on behalf of the Titans. We weren't even sure the Titans existed anymore, but figured that since no one was kicking us out of the garage we might as well get some use out of it. "You oughta go ask at the office," I told them.

"There ain't no one in there. Been trying a couple days. Heard you guys back here and thought since there ain't no one in the office ya'll might be in charge." The speaker had dark hair and eyes and looked like he'd probably seen more action that either Flint or myself.

"Not even sure if there's anything left to be in charge of," Flint answered.

"Looks like the pair of you might be in charge of this here garage."

"This isn't ours," I replied.

"Dunno," said the talkative newcomer. "Possession's nine-tenths of the law. It don't look like anybody minds you hanging out here. I'm Hansun"

"It's not ours," I repeated and bent back to work on Felix when Flint chimed in."

"Hold on, Xander? Why shouldn't we claim this outfit for our own? It's not like Haddock's coming back. And no one else's been around to say they want it."

Hansun interrupted, "Xander!? From the Flats?"

"What's that supposed to mean?", I asked.

"I was in Green group." You saved my life. I barely believed Ito's story when she told us what you did.

"Only thing I did was save my own bacon. Everyone else there was just a way for me to make it happen."

"That's not what she said," replied Hansun. Flint smirked and I noticed. He thinks that joke's funny every single time.

"Cut it out," I told him. "You really think we can get an outfit going? And keep it moving?"

"Sounds like you got a couple guys here already willing to sign on," he nodded towards Hansun and his quiet friend. "Why don't we hang your name out on the door? Hero of Copper Flats could probably hire the pick of the litter."

"Shut up," I threw a spanner at him. "How we going to pay them?" Flint just shrugged.

So I told Hansun and his buddy to come back in a week, and feel free to tell anyone he knew that a new unit was starting out of the Titans' old garage. I figured if I couldn't find a job in a week with all these companies collapsing, then I'd have a hard time finding one ever.

Two days later I had a defense contract in my grubby little fists, sold entirely under false assumptions. I never told AquaTech I was a captain running a company, just that I needed a job. They filled the gaps on their own in their minds, and I didn't bother correcting them. One of the guys there had heard about the Flats, recognized my name from some paper's report of the incident, and figured I had an outfit all prepped and ready.

And then "a week later" showed up. That morning we found a crowd of mech pilots idling in front of the Titans' office. I hired them all on the spot and came to regret it later. Not all of them, and not at once, but most of them were a disappointment at some point.
